Overview

What is Employee Monitoring?

Employee Monitoring is the practice of tracking certain workforce activities to understand how the work is actually performed. Generally, organizations use employee monitoring software to collect operational data, including total work time, attendance records, task activity, overtime hours, and insights into shift completion.

While using ethical employee monitoring software, the goal shouldn't be surveillance; rather, it should be operational visibility.

Comparison

Invasive Vs. Non-Invasive Employee Monitoring

🚫
Invasive Monitoring
Invasive monitoring practices are those that ethical companies avoid. The examples for this include:
✕Constant screen recording even when the app is closed
✕Webcam surveillance
✕Hidden monitoring tools that employees are not aware of
✕Secretly tracking employees outside working hours
Using these unethical employee monitoring practices can raise serious legal issues while damaging employee morale and trust within an organization.
✅
Non-Invasive Monitoring
Non-Invasive monitoring practices are those that ethical companies support. The examples for this include:
✓Time tracking (where the user willingly clocks in)
✓Attendance tracking
✓Shift performance tracking
✓Overtime tracking
Talyvn uses only non-invasive monitoring practices to provide companies with reliable operational insights without violating employee privacy.
Ethics & Compliance

Is Employee Monitoring Ethical & Legal?

⚖️
Is Employee Monitoring Ethical?
The answer to this question depends on how employee monitoring is actually implemented. Employee monitoring becomes ethical when organizations follow principles such as
Full transparency
Only work-related tracking
Clear monitoring practices
Limited & necessary data collection
Companies that follow ethical employee monitoring practices ensure that all of their employees understand what is being tracked and why.
🔒
Is Employee Monitoring Legal?
In most countries, an ethical and non-invasive form of employee monitoring is legal. However, it is only legal when done responsibly, as organizations must
inform employees about monitoring
collect only work-related data
avoid tracking data when the user checks out
follow applicable local data privacy laws
To avoid legal and ethical issues of employee monitoring, companies need to be transparent with employees and comply with data privacy laws.
